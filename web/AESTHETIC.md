# Residuum Landing Page — Aesthetic Guide

## Palette

**Dark geological / mineral aesthetic.** The whole palette is built around "stone" — near-black backgrounds (`#0e0e10`) with grey tones named `stone-deep`, `stone-mid`, `stone-light`, etc. It reads like polished obsidian or dark slate.

### Two accent veins running through it:

- **Blue "vein" (`#3b8bdb`)** — used for dividers, glowing lines, and subtle radial halos. These are styled like luminescent mineral veins in dark rock, with glows and gradient fades to transparent. Like a crack of light splitting stone.
- **Moss green (`#6b7a4a`)** — a muted, earthy olive used sparingly. Feels like lichen on rock.

## Typography

Deliberately classical + technical:

- **Cinzel** (serif display font) for headings — gives it an inscribed-in-stone, almost antiquarian gravitas
- **Literata** (literary serif) for body text at light weight (300) — quiet, readable, bookish
- **JetBrains Mono** for code and labels — the technical counterpoint

## Motion

**The motion language is slow and geological.** Everything fades up from below with `translateY` animations, staggered by 120ms between siblings. The hero elements emerge sequentially over ~2.5 seconds. Nothing snaps — it all eases out. There's a subtle SVG noise texture overlaid on the entire page at 3% opacity, giving it a stone-grain feel.

## Overall Mood

Quiet confidence. Anti-hype. It reads like a developer tool that's deliberately rejecting the glossy SaaS landing page aesthetic in favor of something more understated, permanent, and slightly mysterious — like finding something etched into bedrock. The copy reinforces this: "What Remains," principles like "Just use a Hammer," the whole framing of stripping away layers to find what's essential.

It's the visual equivalent of speaking softly in a room full of people shouting.
